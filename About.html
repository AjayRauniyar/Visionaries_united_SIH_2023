OUR
VISION

The primary objective is to outline strategies for the effective use of exhausted coal mines that contribute to environmental rehabilitation, economic revitalization, and community well-being. The approach emphasizes sustainable practices and the transformation of these sites into multifunctional spaces that align with contemporary needs.

India is one of the largest coal-producing countries globally, with significant coal reserves. The major coal-producing states include Jharkhand, Odisha, Chhattisgarh, and Madhya Pradesh. The country has both underground and open-cast coal mines.

Coal India Limited has 318 mines (as of 1st April 2022) of which 141 are underground, 158 opencast, and 19 mixed mines and also manages other establishments like workshops, hospitals, and so on.
