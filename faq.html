Frequently Asked Questions

  1. What is GLIS and Why is it important for the Ministry of Coal?

    2. → GLIS (Geographical Information System) is crucial for the Ministry of Coal, offering spatial data insights for efficient resource management, infrastructure planning, and environmental impact assessment. It aids in emergency response planning and ensures compliance with regulatory guidelines. GLIS also promotes transparency and community engagement by providing accessible information about coal mining activities.

  2. How does IT contribute to GLIS implementation?

    3. → It plays a pivotal role in GLIS (Geographical Information System) implementation by developing and maintaining the software infrastructure. IT professionals create and integrate systems, ensuring seamless data management, efficient spatial analysis, and user-friendly interfaces. They contribute to the ongoing support and customization, enhancing GLIS functionality and ensuring its alignment with the Ministry of Coal's objectives.

  3. Which is preferable monolithic or microservices?

    4. → For our Government and Land Information System (GLIS) website under the Ministry of Coal, a microlithic architecture is preferable. A monolithic structure simplifies development and maintenance, crucial for a government system. Unlike microservices, a monolith avoids unnecessary complexity, ensuring robustness and efficient data management for geospatial and land information, promoting streamlined operations without the need for extensive distributed components.

  4. Why machine learning is preferred over deep learning?

    5. → For our Government and Land Information System (GLIS) website in the Ministry of Coal, utilizing machine learning is advisable due to its practicality and efficiency. Machine learning techniques can enhance data analysis, predict patterns, optimize land use planning, and coal production trends, and automate data processes without the complexity of deep learning.

  5. why did we choose MongoDB over MySQL?

    6. → MongoDB is preferred over SQL for our GLIS website in the Ministry of Coal due to its flexibility with geospatial data and scalability. In GLIS, spatial information like land and coal reserves can be efficiently stored and queried using MongoDB's native geospatial indexes, facilitating location-based queries for better decision-making. Additionally, MongoDB's NoSQL nature accommodates evolving data schemas, crucial for adapting to the dynamic and diverse data sets associated with geospatial information in the Ministry of Coal.

  6. How can stakeholders including businessmen and individuals collaborate with the ministry on this platform?

    7. → Stakeholders, including businessmen and individuals, can collaborate with the Ministry of Coal through our GLIS platform by sharing geospatial insights, contributing data on land use, and participating in coal-related planning. For example, businesses can provide real-time data on coal extraction sites, aiding in sustainable resource management. Individual users can contribute local knowledge, enhancing the platform's accuracy. Such collaboration fosters a comprehensive understanding of the coal landscape, enabling informed decision-making and sustainable practices, aligning with the Ministry's goals for effective resource management.

  7. What security measures are in place to protect sensitive information on website?

    8. → Our GLIS website secures sensitive data through encryption, like a secret code that protects information during transmission and storage. Access controls act like digital locks, allowing only authorized users to access or modify important data. Regular security checks and updates are done to fix any potential weaknesses, ensuring the Ministry of Coal's data stays safe and confidential.

  8. How often is the data updated on the website? What is the website and what is the source of information?

    9. → Data on the GLIS website is updated regularly, typically every month, to ensure the information is current and accurate. The source of information includes government reports, surveys, and data submissions from coal-related stakeholders, providing a comprehensive and reliable dataset for users on the Ministry of Coal's geospatial and land information platform.

  9. How can the government utilize exhausted coal mines using a website?

    10. → Using our GLIS website, the government can plan the transformation of exhausted coal mines into renewable energy sites, like solar farms or wind installations, leveraging geospatial insights for optimal placement. This approach promotes green energy initiatives, diversifies land use, and contributes to sustainable development, aligning with the Ministry of Coal's goals for responsible resource management.

  10. Are there specific maps or visualizations available on the Glis website?

    11. → Yes, our GLIS website offers specific maps and visualizations, such as interactive coal reserve maps, land-use patterns, and suggestions for effective land usage after the coal mine is exhausted. These visuals aid stakeholders, including businesses and policymakers, in making informed decisions about resource allocation, environmental impact assessments, and strategic planning for sustainable coal management, aligning with the Ministry of Coal's commitment to transparency and effective data communication.
